The Model Muslim

A Novel

by Saad Facchine

"If a system promises you a perfect life, what parts of you have to disappear to fit it?"

One Paragraph Pitch

In a Muslim-majority America, every believer carries the Awaz—a sleek algorithm whispering all the 'right' moves. For Kamaal, a celebrated writer-director, the Awaz has dictated his life, including concealing his true identity. When the Caliphate offers his newborn son the first infant Awaz, Kamaal faces a harrowing choice between his curated life and his family's autonomy.

Synopsis

Kamaal has had his Awaz longer than most. Recruited in high school as a test case for the New Caliphate's "opportunity to improve Muslim lives," he became a celebrated writer-director with a seemingly perfect life—a beautiful wife, a newborn son, and a glossy resume. But there's a cost. The Awaz has always told him to keep one truth offstage: he's gay. The device engineered a lavender marriage and dressed it up as virtue.

Now dissent is simmering—people are starting to notice the gap between obedience and joy—and the Caliphate needs a grand gesture to steady the faithful. They offer Kamaal an honor that's also a threat: let your newborn be the first infant fitted with an Awaz. If Kamaal agrees, he hands his son's autonomy to a machine before the boy can even form a memory. But if he refuses, he risks collapsing the curated life the Awaz built for him.

Author Bio

Writing from my lived experience as a gay Muslim working in technology, I bring authenticity and depth to The Model Muslim. I live near Baltimore with my husband and two cats, and unlike Kamaal, I am very happily married. This novel explores the intersection of technology, faith, and identity through a queer lens, drawing from my personal journey navigating these complex spaces.
