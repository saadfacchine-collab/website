The Story

Allah forbid Kamaal make his own decisions. Every choice, down to the teaspoons of sugar in his Friday chai, had to be cleared by the voice inside his skull—the Awaz. The New Caliphate lodged it there years ago, a device feeding him shortcuts for life: how to stand, what to say, even which desires to bury.

And sure, it delivered—buffed him into respectability: the polished career, the dutiful wife, the swaddled newborn. All in the Caliphate's America, where half the country marches to the same script.

But this gift comes with a price. The Awaz buries his queer identity, forces him into a lavender marriage, and parades him as the perfect Muslim he can never truly be.

The New Caliphate isn't done with him either. To crush rising dissent, they plan to fit an Awaz into every newborn—starting with Kamaal's child. Consent means surrendering his son's first breath of freedom. Refusal risks the same fate that swallowed his first love: a body alive but autonomy erased, trapped forever in autopilot.

Author

Saad Facchine is a gay Muslim working in AI and technology. He lives near Baltimore with his husband and two cats. This novel draws from his lived experience navigating the intersection of faith, identity, and technology.
